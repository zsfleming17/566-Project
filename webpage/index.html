Obstructed Seat Detection in MLB Stadiums
Zachery Fleming · Fall 2025

Overview

This project trains a classifier to detect whether a ballpark seat has an obstructed view versus a clear view. The primary goal is a fan-centered definition of obstruction (rails, poles, netting that block the field of play), not transient occlusions like people standing. Methods include transfer learning (MobileNetV3-Small), sanity-check diagnostics (edges/Hough), and interpretability (Grad‑CAM).

Proposal

Problem: detect obstructed seats from photos. Motivation: improve ticket transparency and fan experience. Plan: collect 200–300 initial images, build a baseline with transfer learning, then expand to 400–600 and evaluate cross‑stadium generalization. Timeline detailed below.

Midterm Progress & Results

Dataset

231 photos from four MLB stadiums (AmFam, Wrigley, Oracle, Fenway). Labels: clear vs obstructed. Splits: stratified 70/15/15 and Leave‑One‑Stadium‑Out (LOSO).

Sample images (top: clear, bottom: obstructed).

Model

MobileNetV3‑Small (ImageNet weights), 224×224 inputs, light augmentations, class‑weighted CE loss, AdamW. Early stopping based on validation accuracy.

  • Random split (test, n=35): 83% accuracy; clear R=1.00; obstructed R=0.57.
  • LOSO – Wrigley (test, n=54): 93% accuracy; clear R=1.00; obstructed R=0.85.

Performance varies by stadium; LOSO demonstrates cross‑domain generalization.

Figure: Confusion matrix on random split. Model rarely mislabels clear seats but misses some obstructed ones.
Figure: Confusion matrix for LOSO (Wrigley held‑out). Strong generalization to unseen stadium.
Grad‑CAM overlays (optional at midterm). The model focuses on poles/netting when predicting obstructed.

Challenges & Plan

  • Ambiguity: people standing vs stadium obstructions; partial occlusions that don’t block the field of play.
  • Next: expand to 400–600 photos; refine labels with obstruction_type and blocks_field metadata; evaluate all LOSO folds; add stronger augmentations and Grad‑CAM analysis.
